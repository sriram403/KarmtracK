So, you're here. You've finally admitted it. Your digital life is a hot mess. You've got 87 browser tabs open, bookmarks scattered across three different services, your to-do list is a sticky note clinging to dear life on your monitor, and your "brilliant research" is a bunch of random links in a text file named `stuff.txt`.

## What Fresh Hell is This? (The Features)

Look, it's not going to do your laundry, but it'll make you *feel* like you have your life together. Which is half the battle.

### 🔖 The Hoarder's Paradise (Bookmark Manager)
-   **Chuck a URL at it.** Any URL. It catches.
-   **Tag Your Shenanigans.** Slap tags like `ai`, `memes`, `world-domination-plans` on anything.
-   **"Possible Idea?" Box.** A special place to write down *why the heck you saved this in the first place*. Future you will be grateful.
-   **Magic Previews.** It automagically grabs YouTube thumbnails and, yes, even renders those pesky X/Twitter links into full, glorious tweet cards. (As long as you're online, I'm not a miracle worker).

### ✅ The Procrastinator's Panic Room (Tasks & Calendar)
-   **Kanban, Baby!** A simple `Todo`, `In Progress`, and `Done` board to track your "good deeds" (or just, you know, work).
-   **Calendar View.** See all your looming deadlines in one terrifyingly convenient place.
-   **Edit & Delete.** Changed your mind about "taking over the world" today? Fine. Edit the task or just nuke it from orbit.

### 🧠 The Conspiracy Theorist's Corkboard (Research Hub)
-   **Digital Notes.** A clean space for your manifestos, research, or grocery lists.
-   **Auto-Saving.** Because hitting Ctrl+S is for peasants. You type, it saves.
-   **Delete with Prejudice.** Realized your "AI will replace squirrels" theory was flawed? Get rid of the evidence.

### 🔍 The "Where The Heck Did I Put That?" Button (Global Search)
-   One search bar to rule them all. Type in a word, and it scours your Bookmarks (titles, ideas, AND tags), Tasks, and Notes to find what you lost.

## The Tech Stack (The Stuff That Makes it Go Brrr)

This bad boy was forged in the fires of... well, my laptop. It's a simple, robust stack designed to just plain *work* on your local machine.

-   **Backend:** Node.js + Express
-   **Database:** SQLite (It's just a file, baby! Easy to back up.)
-   **Frontend:** Vanilla JavaScript, HTML5, CSS3 (No bloated frameworks. We're keeping it lean and mean.)

## How to Get This Thing Running (The Setup)

Don't panic. It's easier than assembling IKEA furniture. You'll need [Node.js](https://nodejs.org/) installed on your machine. That's the only prerequisite.

**1. Clone This Pile of Code**
First, you gotta steal it. Open your terminal and run:
```bash
git clone https://github.com/your-username/KarmtracK.git
```

**2. Navigate Into the Lair**
Don't just stand there, get in.
```bash
cd KarmtracK
```

**3. Summon the Internet Gnomes**
This command installs all the necessary bits and pieces. It might take a minute. Go get a chimichanga.
```bash
npm install
```

---

## Let's Light This Candle! (Running the App)

Ready? Just one command.
```bash
node server.js
```

Your terminal will spit out something like `Server running on http://localhost:3000`.

Now open your favorite web browser and go to that address:
**👉 `http://localhost:3000` 👈**

Boom. You're in. Go forth and organize your chaos.

### Backing Up Your Karma
All your precious data—every bookmark, task, and note—lives in a single file: `pkm.db`.

Want to back it up? **Just copy that file somewhere safe.** That's it. Your entire digital brain, saved.
